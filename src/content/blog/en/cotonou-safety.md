It's often the first question people ask before traveling to Benin: **is Cotonou safe?** The short answer: yes. But like any destination, a few practical pointers will help you enjoy your stay with complete peace of mind.

## Benin — A Stable Country in West Africa

Benin is recognized as one of the most stable countries in the region. An established democracy, no internal conflict, and a tradition of hospitality deeply rooted in the culture. Beninese people use the word **"Kwabo"** — welcome in Fon — and it's not just a phrase: welcoming visitors is part of the national identity.

Cotonou, the country's economic capital, is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city. Like any large African city, some neighborhoods are busier than others, but the overall sense of insecurity is significantly lower than what you might experience in some European or American metropolises.

## Safe Neighborhoods in Cotonou

Not all neighborhoods are equal, and your choice of accommodation plays a key role in your experience.

**Fidjrossè** is one of the best choices for travelers. This residential neighborhood, popular with expats and families, offers a calm and secure environment. Close to the beach (10-minute walk), well-served by transport, with restaurants and shops nearby — it's the ideal balance between tranquility and accessibility. Other neighborhoods like **Haie Vive**, **Les Cocotiers**, and **Cadjehoun** are also known for being safe and pleasant.

## Basic Precautions — Same as Anywhere

Common-sense rules apply in Cotonou just as they would in any major city:

- **Don't display valuables** (jewelry, large sums of cash) conspicuously
- **Keep your belongings close** in markets and busy areas
- **Use Gozem** (the local ride-hailing app, similar to Uber) for your trips, especially in the evening — fare displayed upfront, tracked route, in-app payment
- **Avoid poorly lit areas** at night, especially if you're alone
- **Photocopy your documents** and keep originals in a safe

These precautions are the same ones you'd take in any major city worldwide. Nothing specific to Cotonou.

## Getting Around Safely

Transportation is a key aspect of daily safety. In Cotonou, you have three main options:

1. **The Zem** (moto-taxi) — the most common mode of transport. Negotiate the price before getting on and ask for a helmet. Practical and affordable (200 to 500 FCFA per ride).

2. **Gozem** — the local ride-hailing app. It's the safest option: you see the fare before the ride, the route is tracked, and you can share your location. Download the app before you arrive.

3. **Private driver** — your host can arrange a trusted driver for the day (15,000 to 25,000 FCFA). Ideal for excursions or if you prefer the comfort of an air-conditioned car.

## Cotonou at Night

Cotonou has a nightlife, especially on weekends. Restaurants, bars, and maquis (local eateries) are lively and welcoming. For nighttime outings:

- Use **Gozem** for your trips
- Stay in neighborhoods you know
- Go out in groups when possible
- Let your host know your plans — they can recommend the best spots
